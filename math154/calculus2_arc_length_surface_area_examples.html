Example - arc length

Find the length of the line segment given by \(y = 4x + 6 \) from \(x=1\) to \(x = 6\).

  1. First draw the graph.
  2. Calculate the length without integration using known formulas from geometry.
  3. Set up an integral that calculates this length.
  4. Evaluate your integral and verify your integral reproduces the geometric formula calculation.

Set up an integral that represents the arc length of the curve \(y = \frac{1}{2}x^2 + \frac{1}{3}x^3 \) in quadrant II of the Cartesian plane. Example - Catenary

The shape of a hanging cable between two supports is called a catenary. It looks like a parabola, but it is slightly different. Consider a cable hanging between two poles 100 feet apart. We'll put the origin in the middle of the poles. The shape of the cable is given by \( \displaystyle h(x) = 250 \cosh (0.004 x) - 225 \). Then we see \(h(0) = 25\) and \(h(\pm 50) \approx 30\), so there is about a 5 foot sag in the cable. Calculate the length of the cable.


Hyperbolic trig functions
  • \(\displaystyle \cosh(bx) = \frac{e^{bx} + e^{-bx}}{2} \)

  • \(\displaystyle \sinh(bx) = \frac{e^{bx} - e^{-bx}}{2} \)

  • Show \(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\cosh(bx) = b\sinh(bx) \)

  • Show \(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\sinh(bx) = b\cosh(bx) \)

  • Show \(\displaystyle \cosh^2(bx) - \sinh^2(bx) = 1\)

Example - surface area of a sphere

Use the ideas of surface areas of revolution to show the surface area of a sphere with radius \(R\) is \(SA = 4\pi R^2\). Make sure to draw a picture and start with the idea that \(\displaystyle SA = \int_a^b~dSA\).

Example - surface area

Find the surface area of the shape made by revolving the graph of \(y = \sqrt{x}\) about the \(x\)-axis from \(x=0\) to \(x=2\).

Example - surface area of funnel 2 ways

Determine the surface area of the funnel shape made by rotating the curve \(y = x^{1/3}\) about the \(y\)-axis.

  1. Draw a picture of the situation.
  2. Find the surface area by setting up and evaluating an integral with respect to \(x\).
  3. Next set up and evaluate an integral with respect to \(y\).
  4. Verify you calculate the same value for surface area in each case.
  5. Which way was easier to calculate, easier to set up, or easier to understand? Why?
