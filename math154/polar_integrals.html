Integrals in Polar Coordinates

Warmups

  1. Find the polar coordinates \( (r,\theta) \) of the following points with Cartesian coordinates:
    1. \( (1,1) \)
    2. \( (1,0) \)
    3. \( (0,3) \)
    4. \( (-2,0) \)
  2. Find the Cartesian coordinates of \( (2, 3\pi/2)_{r,\theta} \).
  3. Transform the following equations from rectangular to polar coordinates.
    1. \( x^2 + y^2 = 25 \)
    2. \( y = x^2 - x \)
  4. Transform the following equations from polar to rectangular coordinates.
    1. \( r\cos \theta = 2 \)
    2. \( \tan \theta = 1 \)
    3. \( r = 2\cos \theta \)
