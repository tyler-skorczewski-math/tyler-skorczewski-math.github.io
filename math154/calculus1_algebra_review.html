Calc I Algebra Review

Example 1

Consider the three problems below. For each problem, let \(B\) represent Billy's age in 2020, and \(D\) his dad's age in 2020.

  1. Little Billy's father is three times as old as Billy in 2020. Ten years later, Billy's father will only be twice as old as Billy. What year was Billy born, and how old was his dad?

  2. Little Billy's father is three times as old as Billy in 2020. Ten years later, Billy's father is 10 less than three times Billy's age. What year was Billy born, and how old was his dad?

  3. Little Billy's father is three times as old as Billy in 2020. Ten years later, Billy's father is 20 less than three times Billy's age. What year was Billy born, and how old was his dad?


  • Try to solve each problem.
  • Write out any observations you make as you solve these.
  • Think about how these problems could be represented graphically.

Example 2 - parabola

Let \(f(x) = -x^2 - 6x\).

  1. Does the graph of \(f\) open up or down?
  2. What is the vertex of this graph?
  3. What is the equation for the axis of symmetry of the graph?
  4. What are the \(x\) and \(y\) intercepts of the graph?
  5. What is the domain and range of \(f\)?
  6. Sketch the graph of \(f\).

Example 3 - more parabolas

Let \(g(x) = 3x^2 - 24x - 5\).

  • Does \(g\) have a maximum or minimum?
  • Write \(g(x)\) in standard form for a parabola, i.e. \(g(x) = a(x-h)^2 + k\).
  • What is the vertex of the graph of \(g\)?

    Example 6 - Trig identities

    Trigonometric Identities

    1. \(\cos(\alpha \pm \beta) = \cos (\alpha) \cos (\beta) \mp \sin(\alpha)\sin(\beta)\)

    2. \(\sin( \alpha \pm \beta) = \sin(\alpha)\cos(\beta) \pm \cos(\alpha)\sin(\beta)\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \sin^2 (\theta) = \frac{1 - \cos (2\theta)}{2}\) or \(\displaystyle \sin^2 (\theta/2) = \frac{1 - \cos (\theta)}{2}\)

    4. \(\displaystyle \cos^2 (\theta) = \frac{1 + \cos (2\theta)}{2}\) or \(\displaystyle \cos^2 (\theta/2) = \frac{1 + \cos (\theta)}{2}\)

    5. \( \cos^2 (\theta) + \sin^2 (\theta) = 1\)
