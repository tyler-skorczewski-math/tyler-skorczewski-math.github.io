Example 3 - plane

Two points determine a line, and three points determine a plane (provided all three points are not colinear). What is the plane through the points (1,0,1), (1,1,0) and (0,1,1)? What is the same anywhere on the plane?


Generally, the vector equation of a plane is given by \( \vec{n} \cdot ( \vec{r} - \vec{r_0}) = 0\). Explain this equation using a picture with appropriate labels and sentences. Expand this equation to find the scalar and linear forms of equations for a plane.
