Example - Ladder

A ladder 20 feet long leans against a building. The bottom of the ladder is initially 1 foot away from the base of the building. The bottom of the ladder starts to slide away from the building at a rate of 4 feet/sec. How fast is the top of the ladder sliding down the building when the the top is 8 feet above the ground?

  • Draw a picture of this situation. Let \(h\) represent the height of the ladder and \(x\) the distance from the building.

  • What shape(s) are you drawing? What geometric relationships are related to these shapes?

  • Write an equation relating \(x\) and \(h\).

  • Differentiate this equation implicitly with respect to time. Why with respect to time?

  • What variable in your differentiated equation above are we interested in finding? Isolate this quantity using algebra.

  • Substitute values given in the problem (you may need to solve for some!) to determine the answer to the orginal question.

Example - Rocket Launch

In 2014 NASA launched a new Orion rocket. Watch the video below (at least from 50 second mark until the 2 minute mark).

In particular note that at the 28 second mark the video notes that the rocket is moving 1341 feet/sec.

The camera filming this launch is on a hill quite a ways away from the launch. This camera has to rotate to keep the rocket in frame. Let's assume that the camera is 10000 feet away on top of a 1000 foot high hill. The rocket launched from near sea level. We'll also assume that at the 28 second mark the rocket has an altitude of 20000 feet and is traveling vertically. At what rate is the camera rotating to keep the rocket in frame?

Example - resistors

If two electrical resistors are connected in parallel, the total resistance is given by \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{R} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2}\). If \(R_1\) is increasing at a rate of \(0.5 \Omega\) per second and \(R_2\) is decreasing at a rate of \(1 \Omega\) per second, at what rate is the total resistance changing when \(R_1 = 20\Omega\) and \(R_2 = 50\Omega\)?
