Calc I Integrals review

  1. Evaluate the following integrals:
    1. \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/4} \tan x ~dx\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{4-x}{x^2 - 16} ~dx\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{y^3 + y - \sqrt{y}}{y^2} ~dy\)

    4. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{3-z} ~dz\)

    5. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2e^x}{4e^x + 5} ~dx\)

    6. \(\displaystyle \int_4^6 \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^3 + 5}} ~dx\)

    7. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{\sin (\ln (2x))}{x} ~dx\)


  2. Evaluate the following limits:
    1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{e^{-x}}{x^2 + 2x + 6}\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^3 - 2x^2 + 4}{x - \sqrt{3x} - 4x^3}\)

  3. Water is pumped out of a holding tank at a rate of \(5 - 5e^{-0.12 t}\) liters/minute, where \(t\) is minutes since the pumping started. If the holding tank contains 1000 liters of water when the pumping started, how much water does it hold 1 hour later? Is the pumping rate increasing or decreasing? Can you come up with an explanation for this?

  4. A bar of metal is cooling from 1000 degrees C to room temperature, 20 degrees C. The temperature, \(H\), of the bar \(t\) minutes after it starts cooling is given by $$H(t) = 20 + 980e^{-0.1t}.$$
    1. Find the temperature of the bar at the end of one hour.
    2. Find the average value of the temperature over the first hour.
    3. Is your answer to part b greater or smaller than the average of the temperatures at the beginning and end of the first hour? Explain this in terms of the concavity of the graph of \(H\).


  5. An old rowboat has sprung a leak. Water is flowing into the rowboat at a rate \(r(t)\) given in the table below.
    t minutes 0 5 10 15
    r(t) liters/min 12 20 24 16
    Compute an upper and a lower estimate for the amount of water that has flowed into the boat, and draw a figure/graph that illustrates the lower limit.
