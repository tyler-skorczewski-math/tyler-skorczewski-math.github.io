Definitions

There are two definitions for the derivative of a function \(f\) at point \(a\), denoted by \(f'(a) = \frac{df}{dx}(a)\).

  1. \(\displaystyle f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a + h) - f(a)}{h}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle f'(a) = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x) - f(a)}{x-a}\)

Can you explain why these are equivalent?

Let \(h=x-a\). Then \(h \to 0\) is the same as \(x \to a\) and the difference quotients are the same.
