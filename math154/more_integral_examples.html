Example 1 - Weibull distribution

The Weibull distribution is a popular probability distribution when considering lifetimes, \(x\), of animals, machine operating times, and product lifetimes. The Weibull pdf is given by $$f(x) = \frac{\beta}{\theta} \left(\frac{x}{\theta}\right)^{\beta-1} e^{-\left(\frac{x}{\theta}\right)^{\beta}}$$ if \(x \geq 0\) and 0 otherwise. \(\beta \geq 1\) and \(\theta\) are parameters that determine the shape.

  • Use desmos to graph \(f(x)\) for all six combinations of \(\theta = 1, 10\) and \(\beta = 1, 2, 5\). What do you notice about how the two parameters behave?
  • Show that \(f(x)\) is a probability density function.

Example 4 - Joule-Lenz Law

According to the Joule-Lenz Law, the amount of heat generated by an alternating current $$I = I_0 \sin\left(\frac{2\pi t}{T} - \phi\right)$$ flowing in a conductor with resistance \(R\) ohms from \(t=T_1\) to \(t=T_2\) is given $$Q = 0.24R\int_{T_1}^{T_2} I^2 dt$$ joules. Find the amount of heat generated during a cycle from \(t=0\) to \(t=T\).
