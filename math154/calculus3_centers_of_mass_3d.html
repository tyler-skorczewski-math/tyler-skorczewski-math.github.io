  1. \(\bar{x} = \frac{\iiint_R x\rho (x,y,z)~dV}{\iiint_R \rho (x,y,z)~dV} \)

  2. \(\bar{y} = \frac{\iiint_R y\rho (x,y,z)~dV}{\iiint_R \rho (x,y,z)~dV} \)

  3. \(\bar{z} = \frac{\iiint_R z\rho (x,y,z)~dV}{\iiint_R \rho (x,y,z)~dV} \)

Example - Moments of Inertia

The moment of inertia is related to the rotation of the mass. Specifically, it measures the tendency of the mass to resist a change in rotational motion about an axis. The moment of inertia of a particle of mass \(m\) about an axis is \(I = mr^2\) where \(r\) is the distance of the particle from the axis. When we have solids that are not simple point masses we use integrals to calculate moments of interia. \(I_x\) refers to the moment of inertia about the \(x\)-axis.

  • \(I_x = \iiint_Q (y^2 + z^2) \, \rho (x,y,z) \, dV\)
  • \(I_y = \iiint_Q (x^2 + z^2) \, \rho (x,y,z) \, dV\)
  • \(I_z = \iiint_Q (x^2 + y^2) \, \rho (x,y,z) \, dV\)
  1. Find the moments of inertia about each coordinate axis for the box of constant density, \(k\), with length, width, and height \(a,b,c\) in the \(x,y,z\) directions, respectively.
  2. The radius of gyration (kind of like a rotational center of mass) is given by \(\displaystyle R_i = \sqrt{\frac{I_i}{m}} \). Find \(R_x\) for the above box.

Example - Set up Integrals

Set up integrals representing the following quantities below.

  1. The moment of inertia about the \(z\)-axis for the solid cylinder with density \(k\) with \(x^2 + y^2 \leq a^2\) and \(0 \leq z \leq h\).
  2. The center of mass of the hemisphere \(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \leq 1, z\geq 0\) with density \(k\).
  3. The moment of inertia about the \(z\)-axis for the hemisphere above.
  4. The center of mass of solid \(E\) with density \(\rho = 2\). \(E\) lies under the plane \(z = 1 + x + y\) and above the region in the \(xy\)-plane bounded by the curves \(y = \sqrt{x}, y=0, x=1\).
  5. The centroid of the shape \(F\) where \(F\) is bounded by the surfaces \(z = 1-y^2, x+z=1, x=0\), and \(z=0\).
