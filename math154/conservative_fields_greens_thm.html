A question, a definition, and a theorem

Question How do we know when \(\vec{F}\) is a conservative field, i.e. \(\vec{F} = \nabla f\) ?

Definition If \(C\) is an oriented closed curve the line integral of \(\vec{F}\) around \(C\) is called the circulation \( \int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\).

Theorem A vector field is path-independent (conservative) if and only if \( \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = 0\) for any closed curve \(C\).

Prove this theorem.

Green's Theorem

We have seen two ways to determine if \(\vec{F} = F_1 \hat{x} + F_2 \hat{y} \) is not a conservative field, if \( \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} \neq 0\) and/or \(\displaystyle \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y} \neq 0\).

Question Are these quantities related?

Theorem (Green's Theorem) Suppose \(C\) is a piecewise smooth simple closed curve that is a boundary of a region \(R\) in the plane and oriented so that \(R\) is on the left as we traverse \(C\). Let \(\vec{F} = F_1 \hat{x} + F_2 \hat{y}\) be a smooth vector field on an open region containing \(R\) and \(C\). Then \(\displaystyle \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \iint_R \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y}~dA\).

Example - Curl Test

Theorem (Curl Test in 2D) Let \(\vec{F} = F_1 \hat{x} + F_2 \hat{y}\) be a vector field with continuous partial derivatives such that

  1. the domain of \(\vec{F}\) has the property that every closed curve in it encloses a region that lies entirely in the domain of \(\vec{F}\), i.e. no holes, and,
  2. curl \(\vec{F} = 0\).

Then \(\vec{F}\) is a conservative field and \(\vec{F} = \nabla \phi\).
