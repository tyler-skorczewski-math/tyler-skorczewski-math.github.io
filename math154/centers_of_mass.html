Centers of mass examples

Warmups

Example 1 - centers of mass - discrete case

Consider a system created by the collection of points:

  • \(P_1 = (-1,1)\) with mass 3
  • \(P_2 = (2,-1)\) with mass 4
  • \(P_3 = (3,2)\) with mass 8

  1. Plot these points on a set of axes.
  2. What is the total mass of the system?
  3. What is the \(x\)-coordinate of the center of mass of the system? \(y\)-coordinate?
  4. How are the coordinates of the center of mass related to the ideas of weighted averages?

Example 2 - center of mass - continuous

Consider the lamina (thin sheet) bounded by the curves \(x=0, x=1, y=0, y = f(x) = e^x\).

  1. Sketch a picture of the situation.
  2. What is the area of the lamina? When is area equivalent to mass in the centers of mass context?
  3. The moment about the \(y-axis\) for this situation is given by \(M_y = \int_a^b x f(x) dx\). Explain what this formula is describing and why it is specific to this situation. (i.e. what could change that changes the formula?)
  4. Calculate \(M_y\).
  5. Use your explanation above to find a formula for \(M_x\).
  6. Calculate the center of mass of the system.
