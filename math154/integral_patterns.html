Integral patterns

In this lab you will use sage and integration techniques you have learned to calculate the antiderivatives of several functions in the same family of related functions in order to develop some intuition about the form of the antiderivatives of each family.

    1. Determine the antiderivatives of the following rational functions:
      1. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{(x+1)(x+2)}dx\)
      2. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{(x+2)(x-7)}dx\)
      3. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x(x-2)}dx\)
      4. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{(x-3)^3}dx\)
    2. Based on the pattern of your answers above, determine an antiderivative for \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{(x+p)(x+q)}dx\) where\(p\neq q\). What changes do you need to make in the case \(p=q\)?
    3. Use the method of partial fractions to confirm/check your answer above.
    1. Determine the antiderivatives of the following powers of \(x\) times the exponential function:
      1. \(\displaystyle \int xe^xdx\)
      2. \(\displaystyle \int x^2e^xdx\)
      3. \(\displaystyle \int x^5e^xdx\)
      4. \(\displaystyle \int x^7e^xdx\)
    2. From your answers above what do you think the form is for \(\displaystyle \int x^n e^x dx\)?
    3. Check your answer via integration by parts.
    1. Determine the antiderivatives of the following powers of \(x\) times the natural logarithm function:
      1. \(\displaystyle \int \ln x dx\)
      2. \(\displaystyle \int x \ln x dx\)
      3. \(\displaystyle \int x^3 \ln x dx\)
      4. \(\displaystyle \int x^6 \ln x dx\)
    2. From your answers above what do you think the form is for \(\displaystyle \int x^n \ln x dx\)?
    3. Check your answer via integration by parts.
    1. Determine the antiderivatives of the following powers of the cosine function:
      1. \(\displaystyle \int \cos x dx\)
      2. \(\displaystyle \int \cos^3 x dx\)
      3. \(\displaystyle \int \cos^5 x dx\)
      4. \(\displaystyle \int \cos^2 x dx\)
    2. From your answers i-iii above to what do you think the form is for \(\displaystyle \int \cos^n x dx\) where \(n\) is an odd integer? Note that the pattern is different when \(n\) is an even integer.
    3. Check your answer via integration techniques and trigonometric identities.
    1. Determine the antiderivatives of the following functions:
      1. \(\displaystyle \int \sin x \sin 2x dx\)
      2. \(\displaystyle \int \sin 2x \sin 5x dx\)
      3. \(\displaystyle \int \sin 6x \sin 2x dx\)
    2. From your answers i-iii above to what do you think the form is for \(\displaystyle \int \sin (ax) \sin (bx) dx\)?
    3. Check your answer via integration techniques and trigonometric identities.
