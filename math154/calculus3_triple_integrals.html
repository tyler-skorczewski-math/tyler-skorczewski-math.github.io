Example - Cube Mass

A cube with variable density has 1 corner at the origin and side length 4 cm. The density is \(\rho(x,y,z) = 1 + xyz\) gm/cm\(^3\). What is the mass?

Example - volume

  1. Show that the volume, \(V\), of the region between the surfaces \(z=x^2 + y^2\) and \(z = 4\) is \(8\pi\).
  2. Calculate \(\displaystyle \iiint_V z~dV \) where \(V\) is defined above.

Example -

  1. Set up an integral representing the mass of the solid bounded above by \(z=3\) and below by \(z = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}\) with density \(\rho(x,y,z) = z\).
  2. Calculate the mass. What is the best way to do this? (hint: think about Calc II vs. Calc III techniques)
  3. What if the density were \(\rho(x,y,z) = xz\)? Set up integral for mass.

Examples

  1. Set up an integral representing the mass of the solid bounded by the planes
    1. \( z = 1+x\)
    2. \(z = 1- x\)
    3. \(z=0\)
    with \(y \in [0,3]\) and density \(\rho(x,y,z) = 10-z\) gm/cm\(^3\).

  2. Show \( \displaystyle \iiint_T z~dV = \frac{1}{12} \) where \(T\) is the solid region in the first octant bounded by the graphs of \(z=1-x^2\) and \(y=x\).

  3. Show \( \displaystyle \iiint_T \sqrt{x^2 + z^2}~dV = \frac{4\pi}{3} \) where \(T\) is the solid region bounded by the cylinder \(x^2 + z^2 = 1\) and the planes \(y + z = 2,\) and \( y = 0\).

Example - Sketching regions

Let \(f(x,y,z) = \frac{1}{x}\). Suppose \(\displaystyle I = \iiint_V f~dV\) where \(V\) is the region bounded by the plane \(x=1\), and the surfaces \(x=e^z, y = z,\) and \(y^2 + z^2 = 9\). Sketch \(V\) and set up the triple integral for \(I\).
