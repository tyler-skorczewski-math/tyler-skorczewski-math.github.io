Limits of functions of two or three variables

Limits

Informal Definition Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}\). We say the \( \lim_{(x,y)\to(a,b)} f(x,y) = L\) iff we can make \(f(x,y)\) as close to \(L\) as we like by putting \( (x,y) \) close enough to \( (a,b) \), but not exactly \( (a,b)\).

Find the following limits or explain why the do not exist (DNE):

  1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} \frac{xy \cos(y)}{3x^2 + y^2} \)

  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} \frac{xy^2}{x^2 + y^4} \)

  3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} \frac{x^2 \sin^2(y)}{x^2 + 2y^2} \)

  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} (x^2 + y^2) \ln (x^2 + y^2) \)

Continuity questions

  1. Where is \(f(x,y) = \tan (2x + \sqrt{y})\) continuous?

  2. Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to\mathbb{R}\) be given below. What value of \(c\) makes \(f\) continuous at \((0,0)\)? $$f(x,y) = \begin{cases} \frac{x^2 \sin^2(y)}{x^2 + 2y^2}, & (x,y) \neq (0,0)\\ c, & (x,y) = (0,0) \end{cases} $$
  3. Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to\mathbb{R}\) be given below. What value of \(c\) makes \(f\) continuous at \((0,0)\)? $$f(x,y) = \begin{cases} \frac{x^2 + y^2}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + 1} -1}, & (x,y) \neq (0,0)\\ c, & (x,y) = (0,0) \end{cases} $$
