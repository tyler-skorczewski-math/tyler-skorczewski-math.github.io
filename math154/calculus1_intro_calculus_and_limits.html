Example - average versus instantaneous rates of change - motion

Let the distance travelled by a car be described by \(s(t) = 10t^2 + t\).

  1. What is the distance travelled at \(t=0\)?
  2. What is the distance travelled at \(t=10\)?
  3. What is the name given to the average rate of change of \(s\)?
  4. What are the units of the average rate of change of \(s\)?
  5. Calculate the average rate of change of \(s\) from \(t=0\) to \(t=10\).
  6. Calculate the average rate of change of \(s\) from \(t=1\) to \(t=1.1\).
  7. Calculate the average rate of change of \(s\) from \(t=1\) to \(t=1.01\).
  8. Calculate the average rate of change of \(s\) from \(t=1\) to \(t=1.001\).
  9. What do you think is happening at \(t=1\)?

Example tangent line

Consider the curve \(y = f(x) = x^2\). Consider trying to find the equation of the line tangent to this curve at \(x=2\).

  1. What is the equation of the secant line through \( (2,4)\) and \( (3,9) \)?
  2. What is the equation of the secant line through \( (2,4)\) and \( (2.1,f(2.1)) \)?
  3. What is the equation of the secant line through \( (2,4)\) and \( (2.01,f(2.01)) \)?
  4. What is the equation of the secant line through \( (2,4)\) and \( (2.001,f(2.001)) \)?
  5. Draw a picture of the curve and the secant lines above. What are we doing with the lines above?
  6. Can you guess a formula for the tangent line to the curve?

Example distance from velocity

Consider a particle moving along a line with velocity \(v(t) = 10 - |t - 10| \), where \(v\) is measured in m/s and \(t\) is in seconds.

  1. Sketch a graph of \(v(t)\).
  2. When is the particle stopped?
  3. What is the area under the graph from \(t=0\) to \(t = 2\)? to \(t=10\)? to \(t=20\)?
  4. What are the units of area in this situation?
  5. How far has the particle travelled in the first 5 seconds?
