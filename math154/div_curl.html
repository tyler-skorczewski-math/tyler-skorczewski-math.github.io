Example - curl

  1. Calculate the curl of \(\vec{F}\), i.e. \(\nabla \times \vec{F}\) if \(\displaystyle \vec{F}=\begin{bmatrix}x^2y \\ -2xz \\ x + y - z\end{bmatrix}\).
  2. What does curl measure?
  3. Calculate \(\nabla \times \vec{F}\) if \(\displaystyle \vec{F}=\begin{bmatrix}y \\ -x \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).
  4. What do we say when \(\nabla \times \vec{F} = \vec{0}\)?

Example - Mix and Match

Let \(\vec{F}, \vec{G}\) be vector fields and \(f\) be a scalar field.

  1. What is \( \nabla \times (\nabla f) \)?
  2. Assume \(\vec{F}: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3\) has continuous second partial derivatives. Then \(\nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{F}) = 0\). Prove this.
  3. Prove \(\nabla \cdot (f \vec{F}) = f \nabla \cdot \vec{F} + \nabla f \cdot \vec{F}\).
  4. Prove \(\nabla \times (f \vec{G}) = f \nabla \times \vec{G} + \nabla f \times \vec{G}\).
  5. Prove \(\nabla \cdot (\vec{F} \times \vec{G}) = \vec{G} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{F}) - \vec{F} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{G})\).

Example - Cylindrical coords

Consider a vector field given in cylindrical coordinates \(\vec{F}(r,\theta,z) = F_1 \hat{r} + F_2 \hat{\theta} + F_3 \hat{z}\).

\(\displaystyle \nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial (rF_1)}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial F_3}{\partial z}\).

Find the divergence of \(\vec{F} = 2\theta \hat{r} + 3z \hat{\theta} + 4r \hat{z}\).
