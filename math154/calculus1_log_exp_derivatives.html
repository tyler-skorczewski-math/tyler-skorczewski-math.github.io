Definition and Theorem

Let's define \(e\) to be the value such that if \(b=e\) then the graph of \(b^x\) has slope 1 when \(x=0\).

Theorem

\(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}e^x = e^x\)

\(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}b^x = b^x \ln(b) \)

Examples

We can use what we know about \(e^x\) to find rules about \(\ln x\) and other functions.

  1. Sketch the graphs of \(e^x\) and \(\ln x\) on the same axes. Add the line \(y=x\) to this figure. What do you notice?
  2. What is the slope of the line tangent to the graph of \(e^x\) at the point \(\ln a, a\)?
  3. How is the above slope related to the slope of the line tangent to the graph \(\ln x\) at \(a, \ln a\)?

Theorem

Theorem \(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \ln x = \frac{1}{x}\)

\(\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\log_b x = \frac{1}{x} \frac{1}{\ln b} \)

Examples

Find the derivatives of the following functions:

  • \(\displaystyle H(x) = \ln (3x) \)

  • \(\displaystyle J(x) = \ln (x^2 + 4x - 3) \)

  • \(\displaystyle K(x) = \ln (x^7) \)

  • \(\displaystyle L(x) = \ln \left( \frac{4x^2 - 5}{3x - 9} \right) \)

  • \(\displaystyle M(x) = \ln \left( (x^2 - 4)(x^3 + 2x) \right) \)
