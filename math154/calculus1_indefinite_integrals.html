Definite vs Indefinite Integrals

Definite Integrals Indefinite Integrals
\(\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)~dx\) \(\displaystyle \int f(x)~dx\)
has limits of integration no limits of integration
thought of as area under the curve is antiderivative of a function
is a number is a family of functions
\(\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)~dx = F(b)-F(a)\) \(\displaystyle \int f(x)~dx = F(x) + C\)
don't need to consider +C need +C (constant of integration)

Examples -

Evaluate the indefinite integrals below:

  1. \(\displaystyle\int x^2 + \sqrt{x} \, dx\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \int 7\sec\!\left(x\right)\tan\!\left(x\right) \, dx\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \int 11\sin(x)-2\cos(x)\,dx\)

  4. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^5} \, dx\)

Example -

Compute the number of asteroids with diameter between 0.1 km and 100 km.
