Inverse functions

Basic examples

Find the inverses of the following functions:

  1. \( f(x) = 2x +3 \)
  2. \( g(x) = 2^x\)
  3. \( h(x) = \frac{2x}{x+1} \)

Example

Consider \(f(x) = 2x + 3\). Find its inverse and graph both on the same set of axes.

What do you notice about the derivatives of these functions?

Theorem Let \(f\) and \(g\) be inverses of each other. Let \(f(a) = b\). If \(f'(a)\) exists and \(f'(a) \neq 0\). Then \(g'(b)\) exists and \(g'(b) = \frac{1}{f'(a)}\). An equivalent form says then \(g'(x) = \frac{1}{f'[g(x)]}\) provided \(f'[g(x)] \neq 0\).

Consider \(j(x) = (x-3)^3\). What is \(\frac{d}{dx}j^{-1}\) at \(x=8\)?

What about the derivative of inverse tangent?

Examples

Find the derivatives of the following functions:

  1. \(\displaystyle f(x) = \sin^{-1}(x^2) \)

  2. \(\displaystyle g(x) = (1 + \tan^{-1}x)^3\)

  3. \(\displaystyle h(x) = \cos^{-1} (\sqrt{x}) \)

  4. Find the equation of the tangent line to the graph of \(h(x)\) at \(x=0\).

  5. Find \(\displaystyle \frac{dj^{-1}}{dx}(2)\) where \(j(x) = x^2 + 3x + 2\) for \(x \geq -1\).
