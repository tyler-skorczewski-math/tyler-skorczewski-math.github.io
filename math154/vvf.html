Vector valued functions

Example 1 - circular motion

An object moves on a circular path given by $$\vec{r}(t) = a \cos (\omega t) \hat{x} + a \sin (\omega t) \hat{y},$$ where \(\omega = \frac{d\theta}{dt}\) is angular velocity.

  1. Show the velocity is orthogonal to position.
  2. Show the acceleration always points inward.
  3. True or False. The speed of the object is constant.

Example 2 - spacecraft

A spacecraft is flying along a path described by \(\vec{r}(t) = (t^2 - t) \hat{x} + (2 + t) \hat{y} - 3/t \hat{z}\) where \(t\) is in hours. When \(t=3\) the engines are shut down and the ship continues to move. Where is the spacecraft two hours later?

Example 3 - ski park


Ski resorts like to provide interesting courses for their guests. Often these courses include jumps and other obstacles. These jumps must be designed safely or the resort can be held liable for injuries to skiers.

  • https://www.parkrecord.com/news/park-city/hedge-fund-manager-injured-skiing-sues-deer-valley-for-60-million/
  • http://www.seattlepi.com/local/article/Are-some-ski-park-jumps-too-risky-1267313.php

Consider a ski jump where the skier takes off with an initial speed of 10 m/s at an angle of \(\theta = \pi/6\). Assume the jump will be designed so that the skier travels 20 meters horizontally and only gravity is acting on the skier while in flight.

  1. What should the drop in elevation be?
  2. At what angle should the slope be to provide a smooth landing?
  3. Now assume the take off angle is still \(\pi/6\), the gap is 20 meters, and the drop in elevation is 4 meters. What should the initial speed be to clear the gap and what should the angle of the slope be at the other end of the gap?
