Break Even Analysis and other applications

Formulas

  • Slope intercept form of a line: \( y = mx + b\)
  • Point slope form of a line: \( y - y_0 = m (x - x_0) \)
  • Standard form of a line: \( a x + b y = c \)

Example 1 - Computers

A computer manufacturer sells tablets. Its shop has fixed costs of $500 per week. Costs for parts and labor is \\$649 for each tablet. It sells its tablets for \\$899 each. Assume it sells every tablet it makes, and that it makes \(x\) tablets each week.

  1. What is the cost function for each week?
  2. How much revenue is collected each week?
  3. How many tablets does the company need to sell to make a profit?

Example 2 - bagels

Tony plans to open a bagel shop. They estimate weekly costs are

  • rent: $300
  • utilities: $130
  • salaries: $1800
  • insurance: $90
Relevant per bagel values are
  • bagel ingredients: $0.32 per bagel
  • bagel price: $0.95 per bagel

How many bagels do they need to sell to make a profit?

Example 3 - tickets

The Student Center plans to bring a musical group in for a concert. It expects a sellout crowd of 1200 attendees. The group will come for $3000 or $1000 and 30% of the gate.

The Student Center decides on $1000 plus 30% of the gate. How much should they charge for a ticket?

Example 4 - credit cards

Credit card companies aggressively compete for card holders. The percentage of Americans having a credit card has increased with the data shown in the table below.

year % with cc
1989 56
1992 62.4
1995 66.4
1998 67.5
2001 72.7

Consider the following questions:

  1. Can you estimate the percentage with credit cards in 1980, 2000, 2005?
  2. How can you make a line of best fit (use Excel)?
  3. When will 100% of Americans have a credit card?
  4. Is this reasonable?

Example 5 - higher ed expenditures

The US Department of Commerce provides an estimate of expenditures on higher education expenses for 1999-2003.

year expenditure (in billions)
1999 80.0
2000 86.4
2001 95.1
2002 103.9
2003 112.2
  1. Make a line of best fit.
  2. Estimate the expenditures in 2008.
  3. When are expenditures estimated to reach 175 billion?
