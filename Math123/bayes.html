Bayes Theorem

Example 1

Consider two urns that contain balls. Urn 1 has 3 blue and 2 white balls. Urn 2 has 1 blue and 3 white balls. A single six-sided die is rolled and if a 1 or 2 comes up, a ball is drawn from urn 1. Otherwise a ball is drawn from urn 2. If the ball drawn is blue, what is the probability that it came from urn 1? from urn 2?

Example 2

A new, inexpensive skin test is devised for detecting tuberculosis. To evaluate the test before it is put into use, a medical researcher randomly selects 1000 people. Using a precise, but more expensive method already available, it si found that 8% of the 1000 people have TB. Now each of the subjects is given the new skin test and the following results are recorded: The test indicates TB in 96% of theose who have it and in 2% of those who do not. Based on these results, what is the probability of a randomly chosen person having TB given that the skin test indicates the disease? What is the probability of a person not having tuberculosis given that the skin test indicates the disease? What is the probability of a person having TB given that the skin test indicates no disease is present?

Example 3

A company produces 1000 refridgerators a week at three plants. Plant A produces 350 refridgerators a week, plant B produces 250 a week, and plant C produces 400 refridgerators a week. Production records indicate that 5% of the refridgerators from plant A will be defective, 3% from plant B, and 7% from plant C. All refridgerators are shipped to a central warehouse. If a randomly selected refridgerator at the warehouse is found to be defective, what is the probability it came from plant A?

Example 4

If two cards are drawn from a standard 52 card deck without replacement and the second card is a heart, what is the probability the first card is a heart?

Example 5

30,000-49,999 30 43
under 30,000 11 28
  1. What is the probability that a couple selected at random from this area has two incomes?

Example

A creative writing class has 32 students, of whom 16 are seniors, 12 are juniors, and 4 are sophomores. Nine of the seniors, 5 of the juniors, and 2 of the sophomores are journalism majors. A student is selected at random. What is the probability that the student is

  1. a journalism major, knowing that the student is a junior?
  2. not a senior, given that the student is a journalism major?
