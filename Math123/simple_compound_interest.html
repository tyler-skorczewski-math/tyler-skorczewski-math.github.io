Simple & Compound Interest

Formulas

  • Simple Interest
    • interst: \(I = Prt\)
    • amount: \(A = P + I = P(1 + rt)\)
  • Compound Interest
    • amount: \(A = P(1+i)^n = P(1+\frac{r}{m})^n\)
    • interest: \(I = A-P\)
  • Question: What does each variable represent in the equations above?

Example - Simple Discount

A Treasury bill is a government backed loan. Instead of paying interest, they specify the amount paid back, or maturity value, and banks bid on how much to buy them for.

  • Ex. A 26-week T-Bill with a maturity value of $10,000. Suppose a bank bids, and wins, to buy the loan for $9750. What is the rate of return on this investment?
