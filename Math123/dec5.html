Example 1

A large computer company, A, subcontracts the manufacturing of circuit boards to companies B and C, 40% and 60%, respectively. Company B then subcontracts 70% of its orders to company D and 30% to company E. All ship their boards back to A. Quality control at A has found that 0.5%, 1.5%, and 1% of the boards made by C, D, and E, respectively, are defective during the 90 warranty period. What is the probability that a computer sold by A is defective?

Example 2

Consider a box with 3 blue and 2 white balls. We pick out 2 balls one at a time. What is the probability that the second ball is white if we replace the first ball versus without replacement?

Example 3

Consider flipping a coin twice. Show the flips are independent.

Example 4

The National Lung Association surveyed 2000 women. 680 are smokers, and 50 have emphysema. 42 respondents are smokers who have emphysema. Do these results suggest smoking and emphysema are independent events?

Example 5

A single card is drawn from a standard 52 card deck.

  • Let S be the event the card is a spade.
  • Let F be the event the card is a face card.
  • Let R be the event the card is red.

Which pairs of events are independent?

Example 6

A standard 6-sided die is rolled 6 times. What is the probability the sequence of rolls comes up 1,2,3,4,5,6?

Example 7

The probability a battery will last 10 hours is 0.8, and the probability it will last 15 hours is 0.15. Given that a battery has lasted 10 hours, what is the probability it will last 5 more hours?

Example 9

Consider owning a landscaping company and bidding on 3 projects. You estimate the chances of winning the bid on projects A, B, and C to be 0.7, 0.6, and 0.5, respectively and that the chances are independent. What is the probability you win

  1. all three bids?
  2. exactly two bids?
  3. exactly one bid?
  4. no bids?
