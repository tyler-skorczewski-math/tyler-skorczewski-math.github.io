More Probability

Example - mutually exclusive events

Let \(E\) and \(F\) be mutually exclusive events. Suppose that \(P(E)=0.1\) and \(P(F)=0.6\). Find the quantities below.

  1. \(P(E\cap F) \)
  2. \(P(E\cup F) \)
  3. \(P(E^c) \)
  4. \(P(E^c\cap F^c) \)
  5. \(P(E^c\cup F^c) \)

Example - Venn diagram and probability

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be events on sample space \(S\) such that \(P(A)=0.2, P(B)=0.1\), and \(P(A\cap B) = 0.05\).

  • Draw a Venn Diagram labled with probabilties.
  • Find \(P(A \cup B) \).
  • Find \(P(A^c \cap B^c) \).
  • Find \(P(A^c \cap B) \).

Example - random number

Three numbers are drawn from {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7} and placed in a row in the order drawn to form a three-digit number. What is the probability that

  1. the number is 456?
  2. the first digit is 2, 4, or 6?
  3. it is larger than 600?
  4. it is less than 326?

Example - HS reunion

Lauren attended her 10th high school reunion. Of the 65 people present, she observed that 31 were married and in their first marriage, 22 were married and in their second marriage, and 12 were currently single. (a) (b) (c)

  1. If one person is selected at random from the group, find the probability that it is a single person.
  2. If two people are selected at random, find the probability that both are in their second marriage.
  3. If three people are selected at random, find the probability that two are in their first marriage and one is single.

Example - Landscaping company

Consider owning a landscaping company and bidding on 3 projects. You estimate the chances of winning the bid on projects A, B, and C to be 0.7, 0.6, and 0.5, respectively. You also know the chances of winning each are independent. Draw a tree diagram to help you determine the probabilty that you win:

  1. all three bids?
  2. exactly two bids?
  3. exactly one bid?
  4. no bids?

  1. What is the probability that a couple selected at random from this area has two incomes?

Example
