Example 1 - Laboratory Diets

You are working in a laboratory that does experiments with animals. The animals in one particular experiment are to be kept on a strict diet of 20 grams of protein and 6 grams of fat each day. There are two food mixes the lab uses. Mix \(A\) has 10% protein and 6% fat. Mix \(B\) has 20% protein and 2% fat. Your task is to determine how many grams of each mix should be used to obtain the right diet for a single animal.

  1. What are the unknowns in this problem?
  2. How many equations do you need? What do they represent? Units?
  3. What are the equations?
  4. Draw the graphs for these equations in one figure.
  5. Solve the equations you've set up.

Example 3 - Gifts Galore

Gifts Galore Catalogue Company estimates the demand for TVs to be $$p = -10x + 640,$$ where \(x\) is demand quantity and \(p\) is price. The supply equation is $$p = 12.5x + 325,$$ where \(x\) is supply quantity and \(p\) is price.

  • Explain the supply and demand trends exhibited by the equations.
  • Find the equilibrium price.

Example 4 - GG part 2

What if the Gifts Galore company saw demand for a waffle iron is $$p = -2.5x + 148,$$ and the supply is $$p = 1.7x + 43.$$ What is the equilibrium point?

Example 5 - GG part 3

Now the Gifts Galore company has data indicating that when the price is $138, the demand is 72 and the supply 96. The equilibrium point occurs when the price is $120 and the quantity is 88. Find the equations for the supply and demand.

Example 6 - Lean manufacturing

Lean manufacturing is a corporate philosophy or operation strategy built around eliminating waste. This includes waste in inventory, production, and labor.

Consider a factory that makes down jackets, vests, and comforters. A jacket requires 3 lbs of down, a vest 2 lbs, and a comforter 4 lbs. A jacket takes 2 hours of labor to make, a vest requires 1 hour of labor, and a comforter also requires 1 hour. The profit from jackets and vests are $6 each, and the profit from a comforter is $2.

Each week the factory has exactly 600 pounds of down, 275 available manhours and wants a weekly profit of $1150. How many jackets, vests, and comforters should it produce in order to eliminate any excesses in down and labor hours?

Example 7 - bookstore

The bookstore bought a total of 1000 homecoming t-shirts and sweatshirts. T-shirts cost $7.80 and sold for $13.50 each. White sweatshirts cost $16.50 each and sold for $28.00 each. Blue sweatshirts cost $18.00 each and sold for $30.00 each. Total costs at the end of the day were $11,505 and total revenue was $19,600. How many of each item were sold?

Example 8 - movie theater

A 350 seat movie theater charges \(\$10.50\) admission for adults and \(\$7.50\) for children. If the theatre is full for a show and \(\$3411\) is collected, determine how many of each type of ticket was sold.

  1. Identify the variables/unknowns that we need to find.
  2. Write a system of equations that govern this problem.
  3. How many possible ways are there to sell the tickets that earn the revenue given above?

Example 9 - Library

The public libary is budgeted for fiction, nonfiction, and reference books as follows:

  • Purchase 500 books a month for a total of \(\$19,500\).
  • Purchase 50 more fiction than reference books.
  • The average cost of fiction books is \(\$30\); nonfiction books are \(\$40\); and reference books are \(\$50\).

How many of each type of book should the library acquire each month?

  1. Identify the unknowns.
  2. Write as a system of linear equations.
  3. Use substitution, elimination, or row operations to solve the system.
  4. How many ways are there to satisfy the budget above?

Example 10 - brokerage

A brokerage firm has a mutual fund consisting of three stocks, X,Y,Z. On March 1, the closing prices of each were

  • X: $44,
  • Y: $22,
  • Z: $64.
The mutual fund had a total value of $20,480. Three weeks later these figures were
  • X: $42,
  • Y: $28,
  • Z: $62,
with the total value at $20,720. Two weeks after that the figures were
  • X: $42,
  • Y: $30,
  • Z: $60,
with the total value at $20,580.

If the brokerage firm didn't buy or sell any shares, how many of each stock did they own?

Example 11 - car rental company

Mid-size cars cost $27,000, and full-size cost $36,000. They want to buy 100 cars with twice as many compacts as mid-size. How many of each should they buy?
