Functions and Lines

Formulas

  • Slope intercept form of a line: \( y = mx + b\)
  • Point slope form of a line: \( y - y_0 = m (x - x_0) \)
  • Standard form of a line: \( a x + b y = c \)

Example 1 - Busses

A bus rental company charges $100 plus $5 per rider. The maximum occupancy of the bus is 40 people.

  1. Make a table with numbers of riders and cost to rent. (You don't need to include every number.)
  2. Come up with an algebraic definition of the function describing the rental cost.
  3. Sketch a graph of this function.
  4. What are the domain and range of this function?

Definition - Function

Definition: A function is a rule for assigning to each member of a set, called the domain, one and only one member of another set, called the range.

Example 3 - recycling

The city of Cranfills Gap implemented a successful recycling program. Records show solid waste generated annually can be approximated

  1. \(y = -125 x + 4590\), or
  2. \(w = -125 t + 4590\).

Here

  • \(y, w \equiv \) solid waste (tons), and
  • \(x,t \equiv\) years since program began.

Consider the following questions:

  1. What are the independent and dependent variables?
  2. Find the amount of waste in year 6 of the program.
  3. When will the waste decline to 3000 tons?
  4. How much does the waste decline?

Example 4 - cigarettes

The US Department of Health conducted a survey from 1985 to 2008 on the use of cigarettes. Data indicate $$y = -0.47t + 47.1$$ for the 18-25 year old cohort, where \(t\) is the years past 1980 and \(y\) is the percentage of smokers.

Consider the following questions:

  1. What are the independent and dependent variables?
  2. Estimate the percentage of smokers in 2010, 2017, 2027.
  3. When is \(y=20\)? What does this mean?
  4. Graph the function and describe its shape.
  5. Rewrite this equation in point-slope form with \(t_0 = 2\).
  6. Rewrite in standard form.
  7. Make a plot in Excel showing this relationship.
