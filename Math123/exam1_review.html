Exam 1 review

  1. What is the equation of the line through points (5,4) and (10,24)?

  2. I have 80 books in my office. Three years ago I had 59 books in my office. Assuming a linear trend, how many will I have 5 years from now? What is the equation of this line?

  3. Tony plans to open a bagel shop. He estimates his weekly costs are:
    • rent: $300
    • utilities: $130
    • salaries: $1800
    • insurance: $90
    Bagel ingredients are $0.32 per bagel and the price of a bagel will be $0.95. How many bagels does Tony need to sell each week to make a profit?

  4. Write down the augmented matrix for the system of equations below.
    \( \begin{align*} 6 x_1 + 4x_2 - x_3 &= 0\\ -x_1 + x_2 -x_3 &= 7 \\ 5x_1 \hspace{1.57cm} &= 15 \end{align*} \)

  5. The augmented matrix below represents three equations in the variables x,y, and z. Write them down. Is there a solution to this system?
    \(\left[ \begin{array}{c c c | c} 4 & 6 & -1 & 9 \\ 3 & 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]\)
    What if it is changed to this matrix?
    \(\left[ \begin{array}{c c c | c} 4 & 6 & -1 & 9 \\ 3 & 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array} \right]\)

  6. The snack shop makes three mixes of nuts in the following proportions:
    • Mix I: 7 pounds peanuts, 5 pounds cashews, and 1 pound pecans;
    • Mix II: 3 pounds peanuts, 2 pounds cashews, and 2 pound pecans;
    • Mix III: 4 pounds peanuts, 3 pounds cashews, and 3 pound pecans.
    The shop has 67 pounds of peanuts, 48 pounds of cashews, and 32 pounds of pecans in stock. Set up a system of equations to determine how much of each mix the store can produce. You do not need to solve this system.

  7. Raul is on a carefully supervised diet that causes weight loss of 3 pounds per week. After 14 weeks, his weight is 196 pounds. Does the information provided give two points or a point and a slope? Write Raul's weight as a linear function of weeks on the diet.

  8. The Catalog Store found that the demand equation for its Belgian waffle iron was \(p = -2.5x + 148\), where \(x\) is the demand quantity (in thousands) and \(p\) is the price. The supply equation is \(p = 1.7x + 43\), where \(x\) is the supply quantity (in thousands) and \(p\) is the price. Find the equilibrium solution.

  9. You are working in a laboratory that does experiments with animals. The animals in one particular experiment are to be kept on a strict diet of 20 grams protein and 6 grams fat each day. There are two food mixes the lab uses. Mix A has 10% protein and 6% fat; mix B has 20% protein and 2% fat. Your task is to determine how many grams of each mix should be used to obtain the right diet for a single animal.
