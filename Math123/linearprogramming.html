Example - Oil refinery (Sys Ineq)

The maximum daily production of an oil refinery is 2300 barrels. It makes 3 kinds of fuel: gasoline, diesel, and heating oil. Gasoline has a production cost of $6 per barrel. Diesel has a production cost of $5 per barrel, and heating oil has a production cost of $8 per barrel. The daily production budget is $16400. Can you write inequalities governing the number of barrels of each fuel produced?

Example Novelty Store

Ace Novelty wishes to produce two types of souveniers, A and B. Each type A souvenier has a profit of $1 and each type B $1.20. To manufacture a type A souvenier requires 2 minutes on machine I and 1 minute on machine II. To make a type B requires 1 minute on machine I and 3 minutes on machine II. There are 3 hours available on machine I and 5 hours on machine II. How many of each type of souvenier should Ace Novelty make to maximize profit?

Example - Conference planning

The Mathematical Association of America (MAA) plans a 3-day conference at a big hotel with many conference rooms. The conference knows that they need 15 rooms for the first day, 28 for the second day, and 19 for the third day. The hotel rents rooms at a rate of $17 per room for a single day rental, or $30 to rent a room for consecutive days. Can you set up a linear programming problem to figure out how the MAA should rent rooms to minimize the rental cost? You do not need to find a solution. (hint: think about how many ways there are to rent rooms for a single day and consecutive days.)

Example - Mining production scheduling

A mining company operates two mines, each of which produces three grades of ore. The West Summit mine can produce 2 tons of low-grade ore, 3 tons of medium-grade ore, and 1 ton of high-grade ore per hour of operation. The North Ridge mine can produce 2 tons of low-grade ore, 1 ton of medium-grade ore, and 2 tons of high-grade ore per hour of operation. To satisfy existing orders, the company needs at least 100 tons of low-grade ore, 60 tons of medium-grade ore, and 80 tons of high-grade ore. The cost of operating each mine varies, depending on the conditions encountered while extracting the ore.

  1. If it costs $400 per hour to operate the West Summit mine and $600 to operate the North Ridge mine, how many hours should each mine be operated to supply the required amounts of ore and minimize production costs? What is the minimum production cost?
  2. Discuss the effect on the production schedule and the minimum production cost if it costs $300 per hour to operate West Summit and 700 per hour to operate North Ridge. Assume everything else stays the same.
  3. What if it cost $800 per hour to operate West Summit and $200 per hour to operate North Ridge?

Example - Water ski resource allocation

A manufacturing company makes two types of water skis, a trick ski and a slalom ski. Trick skis require 6 hours of fabricating work and 1 hour of finishing work. Slalom skis require 4 hours of fabricating and 1 hour of finishing. There are 108 labor hours avaiable for fabrication and 24 available for finishing each day.

  1. If the profit on a trick ski is $40 and the profit on a slalom ski is $30, how many of each type of ski should be made each day to realize a maximum profit? What is this profit?
  2. Discuss the effect of having the profit on a slalom ski decrease to $25.
  3. Discuss the effect of having the profit on a slalom ski increase to $45.

Example - Syrup Manufacturing

A syrup manufacturer blends 14 gallons of maple syrup and 18 gallons of corne syrup to make Maple Flavored Syrup and Taste O' Maple syrup. Each gallon of Maple Flavored Syrup has 0.3 gallons of corn syrup and 0.4 gallons of maple syrup. Each gallon of Taste O' Maple has 0.4 gallons of corn syrup and 0.2 gallons of maple syrup. Water and other stuff make up the rest of each gallon. The company makes a profit of$4.50 per gallon of Maple Flavored Syrup and $3.00/gal Taste O' Maple. How many gallons of each should they make to maximize profit?

Bonus How much should the company spend to buy another gallon of maple syrup?

Example - Book fair

The Book Fair sponsors book fairs at elementary schools and sells good books to children that are written to capture their interest. In preparation for a fair they put together three different packages of books. Each package contains short story books, science books, and history books. Pack I contains three short story books, one science, and two history books. Pack II contains two short story books, four science books, and one history book. Pack III contains one short story book, two science, and three history books. The Book Fair has 660 short story books, 740 science books, and 853 history books. The profit on each pack is $1.25 for Pack I, $2.00 for Pack II, and $1.60 for Pack III. How many of each pack should be made to maximize profit?

Example - Iron grate production

Kane manufacturing has a division that produces two models of iron grates, A and B. To produce a model A grate requires 3 lbs of cast iron and 6 minutes of labor. To produce a model B grate requires 4 lbs of cast iron and 3 minutes of labor. The profit for each model A is $2.00 and for each model B is $1.50. Every day the company has 1000 lbs of cast iron available and 20 hours of labor. Currently, the company has a cap of 180 model A grates per day. How many of each type of grate should be made to maximize profit?

More questions:

  • The shadow price of a constraint is the increase in profit from a 1 unit increase in the constaint available (for a standard max problem). What is the shadow price of cast iron for this situation.
  • How much can the amount of cast iron change before we have a new optimal solution (from original situation)?
  • How much would the profit from model A grates need to change before a new corner of the feasible set describes the optimal solution?
