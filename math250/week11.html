  1. Calculate \(T \left( \begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix} \right) \)
  2. Calculate \(T \left( \begin{bmatrix}0\\1\end{bmatrix} \right) \)
  3. Calculate \(T \left( \begin{bmatrix}2\\1\end{bmatrix} \right) \)
  4. Calculate \(T \left( \begin{bmatrix}1\\-2\end{bmatrix} \right) \)
  5. Which vectors above are eigenvectors? What are their associated eigenvalues?

Example 3 - Isle Royale


Consider Isle Royale in Lake Superior. On Isle Royale there are wolf and moose populations. This nearly closed system provides an ideal setting for studying the ecology between predator and prey species. Let \(w_n\) be the wolf population in year \(n\) and \(m_n\) be the moose population in year \(n\). Assume the population dynamics behave according to the equations below. $$ w_{n+1} = 0.72 w_n + 0.24 m_n$$ $$ m_{n+1} = -0.16w_n + 1.28 m_n$$

  1. Explain the dynamics using common language.
  2. Write the system in matrix vector form \(A\vec{x} = \vec{b}\).
  3. What are the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix \(A\)?
  4. Theorem Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix. Let \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, ... , \lambda_n\) be the distinct eigenvalues of \(A\) associated with eigenvectors \(\vec{v}_1, \vec{v}_2, ... , \vec{v}_n\). Then the set of eigenvectors are linearly independent.
  5. What does the theorem above mean for our problem.
  6. Assume the population in year 0 is \(\begin{bmatrix}130\\210\end{bmatrix}\). What is the population in year 3?
  7. What happens as \(n \to \infty\)? Are these populations stable?
