Example 1 - Fish populations

Consider a population of fish in a lake. One of the most common starting points biologists use to model a population in a closed environment is the logistic equation: $$ \frac{dP}{dt} = kP \left( 1 - \frac{P}{N}\right). $$ Here, \(P(t)\) is the population at time \(t\), and \(k,N > 0 \) are the intrinsic growth rate and population carrying capacity, respectively.

  1. Explain why \(N\) is called the carrying capacity. Use both mathematical and biological reasoning.
  2. Make a slope field using dfield (dfield.jar). Let \(k=0.1\) and \(N=10\). Plot a few solutions and explain what is happening.
  3. Label this equation using vocabulary from the course.
  4. Use the fact that this equation is separable and find a general solution for the DE. You can assume \( 0 < P < N\) when solving.
  5. Let \(P(0) = P_0\) and find a particular solution.
  6. Now assume that we introduce some harvesting, in the form of fishing. Let the harvesting occur at a constant rate, \(h\). How should we modify our DE?
  7. Find any equilibrium solutions to the new DE.
  8. Make new slope fields with some values of \(h\). How does increasing \(h\) affect the population?

Example 2 - Tanks

  1. Consider a 100 gallon tank filled with saltwater. We want to flush out the salt and replace it with fresh water. We will accomplish this by pouring in pure water at a rate of 5 gal/min and letting the mixture flow out at 5 gal/min. Assume the mixture in the tank is always perfectly mixed.
    1. If there is initially 10 lbs of salt in the tank how much is there at time \(t\)?
    2. How long until there is less than 0.1 lbs of salt in the tank?
  2. Now consider a 50 gallon tank of pure water. Saltwater with concentration 2 lbs/gal is pumped in at a rate of 3 gal/min, is continuously mixed, and flows out at 3 gal/min.
    1. Set up an IVP describing this situation.
    2. Describe the DE using vocubulary we have learned thus far.
    3. Solve this system. Is there a particular method that is aimed at this type of problem?

Example 3 - Drugs in the bloodstream

  1. Come up with a system of differential equations that describe this system. Let \(A\) be the amount of drug in the pill and \(C\) be the amount in the bloodstream.
  2. Let \(A(0) = A_0\) be the initial amount in the pill. Solve your IVP for \(A(t)\).
  3. Use your solution above to find \(C(t)\).
  4. Perform some sanity checks on your solution for \(C(t)\). Do the units work? Is \(C(t) \ge 0\)? Should it be?
  5. Sketch a graph of \(C(t)\).
  6. When is the amount of drug in the bloodstream at a maximum?

Example 4 - Pies


Imagine an apple pie just coming out of a 375 degree oven and resting on the counter in a kitchen at 75 degrees. We can't eat it until it has cooled down to 100 degrees. Let's use Newton's Law of Cooling with insulation constant 4/min to find the time it takes for the pie to cool.
  1. Explain Newton's Law of Cooling in your own words.
  2. Write down the IVP that applies to this situation?
  3. Solve the IVP.
  4. Use your solution to determine the time it takes for the pie to have cooled to 100 degrees.
