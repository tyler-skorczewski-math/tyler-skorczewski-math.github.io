Example 3 - Turtle populations


One way to model a population is to consider the number of individuals in certain stages of development. In Crouse et al. they break up the loggerhead turtle population into seven distinct stages. They are eggs & hatchlings (together), small juveniles, large juveniles, subadults, novice breeders, 1st year remigrants, and mature breeders. We can then keep track of the population in year \(n\) with the vector \(x_n = [x_1, x_2, ..., x_7]^T\) where \(x_1\) are eggs & hatchlings, \(x_2\) are small juveniles, up through \(x_7\) as the mature breeders.

The matrix,\(L\), below is called a Leslie matrix. It can be used to model the dynamics of the population. We say the population behaves so that \(x_{n+1} = Lx_n\). $$L = \begin{bmatrix} 0& 0& 0& 0& 127& 4& 80\\ 0.6747& 0.7370& 0& 0& 0& 0& 0\\ 0& 0.0486& 0.6610& 0& 0& 0& 0\\ 0& 0& 0.0147& 0.6907& 0& 0& 0\\ 0& 0& 0& 0.0518& 0& 0& 0\\ 0& 0& 0 &0& 0.8091& 0& 0\\ 0& 0& 0& 0& 0& 0.8091& 0.8089\end{bmatrix}$$

  1. How many equations are represented with \(x_{n+1} = Lx_n\)?
  2. Write the equation for eggs & hatchlings. Can you interpret it using common language?
  3. What does the number 127 represent in entry (1,5)?
  4. What does the number 0.0486 represent in the entry (3,2)?
  5. What does the number 0.8089 represent in entry (7,7)?
  6. Write and explain the equation for mature breeders.
  7. Why do rows 5 and 6 only have one nonzero entry when the others have more than one?
  8. If there are 10,4,7,5,1,1,3 turtles in stages 1-7 respectively (in thousands) this year, how many will there be next year? Is the population growing?

Example 10 - Wheatstone Bridge

The circuit diagram below is called a Wheatsone bridge. It is useful for determining an unknown resistance of an object. When using the bridge to find resistance \(R_x\), resistances \(R_1\) and \(R_3\) are known, while \(R_2\) is adjustable. \(R_2\) is adjusted until the current through the middle branch (from junctions B to D) is equal to zero. Then the resistance \(R_x\) can be found from $$R_x = \frac{R_2 R_3}{R_1}.$$ We will derive this formula.

  1. Use Kirchoff's current law to write equations for the currents \(I_1, I_2, I_3, I_x, I_G\) in junctions B and D.
  2. Use Kirchoff's voltage law to write equations for the currents in loops ABD and BCD.
  3. When the bridge is balanced so \(I_G = 0\) we have 4 linear equations in the unknowns \(I_1, I_2, I_3, I_x\). Use these to derive the formula above.
