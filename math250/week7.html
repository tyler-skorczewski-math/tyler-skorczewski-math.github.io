Warmups

  1. Write the vector \(\vec{t}\) as a linear combination of \(\vec{u},\vec{v},\vec{w}\). $$\vec{t} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\-7\\9\end{bmatrix}, \vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\-2\\2\end{bmatrix},\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}3\\0\\1\end{bmatrix},\vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\\2\end{bmatrix}$$
  2. True or False. If three vectors \(\vec{a},\vec{b},\vec{c}\) are coplanar then they must be linearly dependent. Justify your answer.
  3. Are the following vectors linearly independent? What is the span of the set of these three vectors? $$\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\2\end{bmatrix} \right\}$$

  6. Find the basis for the subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^4\) that describes all vectors of the form \( (a,b,c,d) \) for which \(a=3c\) and \(b=4d\).
  7. Describe the span of the set of vectors below. $$\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}2\\1\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}-1\\-1/2\end{bmatrix} \right\}$$

Example 2 - Nullspaces or kernels

Consider the matrix \(A\) below. $$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 2 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 3 & 9 & 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}$$

  1. What equation do we solve to find the nullspace, or kernel, of \(A\)?
  2. What kinds of objects are in null\( (A)\) = ker\( (A) \)?
  3. Find RREF of \(A\).
  4. How many free variables are there?
  5. Write your solutions as a linear combination of vectors.

Example 3


Consider the ODE \(dy/dx + P(x) y = 0\).

  1. Label this DE.
  2. Is \(y = 0\) a solution to this DE?
  3. If \(y_1\) is a solution, is \(a y_1\) also a solution? (\(a \in \mathbb{R}\) )
  4. If \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) are solutions, is \(y = a y_1 + by_2\) also a solution?
  5. Are solutions to this ODE a vector space?

Example 4 -

Consider the set of all parabolas $$ \mathcal{P}_2 = \left\{ y(x) | y(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \mbox{, where } a,b,c \in \mathbb{R} \right\}.$$

  1. Show \(\mathcal{P}_2\) is a vector space. Assume normal rules for function addition and scalar multiplication.
  2. Is the set \( \{1,x,x^2\} \) a basis for \(\mathcal{P}_2\)?
  3. What is the dimension of \(\mathcal{P}_2\)?
  4. Is the set \( \{ 1+ x, x^2 + 1, 3x^2 + 2x \} \) linearly independent? Could it be a basis?

Example 5 -

Consider the vector space of all 2x2 symmetric matrices. $$V = \left\{ A_{2\times 2} \mid A = \begin{bmatrix}a & b \\b & d \end{bmatrix}, a,b,d \in \mathbb{R} \right\}$$

Note that in this situation matrices are the objects in the vector space, so a matrix is a "vector" here when thinking about definitions and theorems regarding vector spaces.

  1. Assuming normal rules for scalar multiplication and matrix addition show \(V\) is a vector space.
  2. Show that the set of "vectors" below are a basis for \(V\), i.e. show they span \(V\) and are linearly independent. $$ \mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}$$
  3. What is the dimension of the space?

Example 6 -

Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 \\-4 & 1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\).

  1. Find null\( (A) \), i.e. find the solution set to \(A \vec{x} = \vec{0}\).
  2. What is the basis for null\( (A) \)?
  3. Find a solution to \( A\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}-5\\9\end{bmatrix}\).
  4. Choose two vectors, \(\vec{x}_1\) and \(\vec{x}_2\), in the nullspace of \(A\). Does \(\vec{x}_1\) plus your solution above also solve the equation above? What about \(\vec{x}_2\) plus your solution above?
  5. Are solutions to \(A\vec{x} = \vec{b}\) a vector space?
