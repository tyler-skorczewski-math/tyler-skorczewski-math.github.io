Math 250 - Vector Spaces

Warmups

  1. Let \(\vec{u},\vec{v}, \vec{w}\) be in vector space \(V\), and let \(r,s\) be scalars with vector addition and scalar multiplication defined in the usual way. What are the names given to the following properties?
    • \(\vec{u} + \vec{v} = \vec{v} + \vec{u}\)
    • \(\vec{u} + (\vec{v} + \vec{w}) = (\vec{u} + \vec{v}) + \vec{w}\)
    • \(\vec{u} + \vec{0} = \vec{0} + \vec{u} = \vec{u}\)
    • \(1 \vec{u} = \vec{u}\)
    • \(\vec{u} + (-\vec{u}) = \vec{0}\)
  2. Give the three different distributive properties of vector spaces.
  3. What is the length of vector \( \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\5\\-3\end{bmatrix}\)?
  4. True or False. \(\vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\3\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\2\end{bmatrix}\) are linearly dependent.

Example 1 - Linear combinations

Write the vector \(\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}\) as a linear combination of \(\begin{bmatrix}5\\7\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix}2\\3\end{bmatrix}\).

Example 2 - Linear independence?

The book defines that three vectors \(\vec{u},\vec{v},\vec{w} \in \mathbb{R}^3\) are linearly dependent if and only if there exist scalars \(a,b,c\) not all zero such that \(a\vec{u} + b\vec{v} + c\vec{w} = \vec{0}\). It then says that the vectors are linearly independent if they are not linearly dependent. What is a definition of linear independence, i.e. what is the negation of the above definition?

Determine if the set of vectors \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ 2\end{bmatrix} \right\} \) are linearly independent or linearly dependent.

Example 3 - Quantum mechanics


In quantum mechanics particles have a property analagous to intrinsic angular momentum called spin. The things that make up ordinary matter (electrons, protons, etc.) all have spin 1/2. Spin 1/2 particles can be thought of as a mix of spin up \(\begin{bmatrix} 1/2 \\ 1/2\end{bmatrix}\) and spin down \(\begin{bmatrix} 1/2 \\ -1/2\end{bmatrix}\). Consider a particle \(\chi_1\) in the spin state \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\). Find a linear combination of spin up and spin down that reprents this state, i.e. find \(a,b\) such that \( a \begin{bmatrix} 1/2 \\ 1/2\end{bmatrix} + b \begin{bmatrix} 1/2 \\ -1/2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\). Note that \(a\) and \(b\) are related to the probabilty of observing the particle in that state.

Example 4 - vector subspaces

Determine if the following are vector subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

  1. The half plane \(x=0,y\ge 0, z \in \mathbb{R}\).
  2. The line \(x = 3t, y = 3t + 1, z = 5t - 1, t \in \mathbb{R}\)
  3. The line \(x = t, y = 2t, z = 3t, t \in \mathbb{R}\)
  4. The point \(x=y=z=0\).
  5. The plane \(x + y + z = 0\).

Example 5 - Solution subspaces

Solve the set of equations below and show that the solutions are a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^4\). $$x_1 + 3x_2 - 3x_3 + 7x_4 =0$$ $$x_2 - 4x_3 + 5x_4 =0 $$.
